Wearable Cancer Therapy Device

A Macrofluidic Device for Localized Immunotherapy

View Final Report Watch Video Demo

Project Overview

This was my capstone project, where I led the engineering of a wearable, macrofluidic device designed to enhance the efficacy of cancer immunotherapy. The device allows for the controlled culture and delivery of T-cells directly to the tumor site, addressing the challenges of low cell retention and short-lived therapeutic effects seen in traditional intravenous therapies.

Project Breakdown

Macrofluidic Chip Design & Simulation

Designed the chip in SOLIDWORKS for T-cell culture and simulated fluid dynamics to ensure optimal delivery.

Electronics & Custom PCB

Developed a breadboard circuit and subsequently a custom PCB to control the device's entire electronic system.

App Development & Wi-Fi Communication

Created an Android app for remote management and programmed the microcontroller (C++) for Wi-Fi communication.

MATLAB Modeling & Regression Analysis

Used MATLAB to model cell growth and optimize treatment schedules. Calibrated the pump system with regression analysis.
